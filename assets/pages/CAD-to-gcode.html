Guide to Using AstroPrint to Convert STL to G-code for 3D Printing

AstroPrint is a user-friendly cloud-based platform that simplifies preparing and managing 3D prints. Here's a step-by-step guide to turning your STL file from Tinkercad into G-code using AstroPrint.

What is G-code?

G-code is the language that 3D printers understand. It contains a series of commands that tell the printer how to move, where to place the nozzle, how much filament to extrude, and the temperature settings for the nozzle and bed. Essentially, it translates your 3D model (STL file) into instructions the printer can execute.

Steps to Convert STL to G-code Using AstroPrint:

Export Your STL File from Tinkercad

Log in to Tinkercad and open your design.

Click on Export in the top menu.

Select .STL as the file format and save it to your computer.

Create an Account on AstroPrint

Go to AstroPrint.com and sign up for a free account if you don't have one.

Log in to your account to access the dashboard.

Upload Your STL File

Navigate to the Design Library section in AstroPrint.

Click on Upload Files and select the STL file exported from Tinkercad.

Prepare the File for Printing

Once uploaded, click on the STL file in your library to open the slicer interface.

Choose your printer model from the list or add your printer's profile manually.

Select the slicing settings

Material: Match the filament type you're using (e.g., PLA, ABS).

Quality: Choose the layer height (e.g., Fine, Standard, Draft)

Infill: Set the density of the object's interior (e.g., 20% for typical prints).

Generate the G-code

After configuring the slicing settings, click Slice to generate the G-code.

AstroPrint will process your file and convert it into G-code, which you can preview on the platform.

Download the G-code

Once slicing is complete, download the G-code file to your computer.

Transfer the G-code to Your Printer

Use a USB drive, SD card, or direct network connection (if supported by your printer) to transfer the G-code to your 3D printer.
